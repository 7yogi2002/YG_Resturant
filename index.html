Diversity: Indian cuisine is incredibly diverse, with regional specialties that vary widely in flavors, ingredients, and cooking techniques. You could focus on a specific regional cuisine like North Indian (known for dishes like butter chicken and naan), South Indian (with items like dosas and idlis), or explore a broader range of dishes from across the country.
Spices: Indian cuisine is renowned for its use of spices, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, cardamom, and chili. These spices are used to create complex flavor profiles and aromatic dishes.
Vegetarian Options: Indian cuisine offers a plethora of delicious vegetarian dishes, making it appealing to vegetarians and vegans. Dishes like paneer tikka, chana masala, and vegetable biryani are popular choices.
Tandoori and Grilled Dishes: Tandoori cooking, which involves marinating meats, seafood, or vegetables in a mixture of yogurt and spices before cooking them in a clay oven (tandoor), is another hallmark of Indian cuisine. Tandoori chicken and naan are classic examples.
Curries: Indian curries come in a variety of flavors and styles, from creamy and mild to spicy and tangy. Popular choices include chicken tikka masala, lamb rogan josh, and palak paneer.
Street Food: Indian street food offers a treasure trove of delicious and affordable options, including chaat (savory snacks), samosas, vada pav, and pav bhaji. Incorporating street food items can add a fun and casual element to your menu.
Desserts: Indian desserts, or mithai, are often rich, sweet, and indulgent. Favorites include gulab jamun, rasgulla, and kulfi (Indian ice cream).

Vegetarian Options:
Paneer Dishes: Paneer is a type of Indian cheese used in various dishes like Paneer Tikka, Palak Paneer (spinach with paneer), and Paneer Butter Masala.
Dal: Lentils are a staple in Indian cooking, and dishes like Dal Tadka, Dal Makhani, and Sambar are popular vegetarian choices.
Vegetable Curries: There are countless vegetable curry options such as Aloo Gobi (potato and cauliflower), Baingan Bharta (roasted eggplant), and Bhindi Masala (okra).
Vegetable Biryani/Pulao: Fragrant rice dishes cooked with vegetables, herbs, and spices.
Vegan Options:
Chana Masala: A flavorful chickpea curry made with tomatoes, onions, and spices.
Aloo Chaat: Spiced potato snack often served as street food.
Vegetable Samosas: Triangular pastry filled with spiced potatoes and peas, typically fried.
Vegetable Pakoras: Deep-fried fritters made with vegetables and chickpea flour batter.
Vegetable Korma: Creamy curry made with mixed vegetables and coconut milk.
Gluten-Free Options:
Tandoori Chicken: Grilled chicken marinated in yogurt and spices.
Raita: Yogurt-based condiment with cucumbers, mint, and spices.
Papadums: Thin, crispy lentil wafers served as an appetizer.
Chicken Tikka Masala: Grilled chicken in a creamy tomato-based sauce.
Fish Curry: Various fish curries cooked with coconut milk or tomatoes and spices.

Fine dining restaurants and those with limited seating often encourage reservations to ensure they can accommodate guests at their preferred times. On the other hand, casual dining establishments may operate on a first-come, first-served basis without reservations.

If you're planning to open a restaurant, whether you choose to take reservations would depend on your restaurant's concept, capacity, and customer preferences. Offering reservations can be a convenient way to manage table turnover and provide a better dining experience for your guests, but it also requires careful planning and coordination to ensure smooth operation

On-site Parking: Some restaurants have their own parking lots or dedicated parking spaces for customers. This is especially common in suburban or rural areas where space is more plentiful.
Street Parking: In urban areas where space is limited, restaurants may rely on street parking for customers. However, availability and regulations regarding street parking can vary widely depending on the location and time of day.
Valet Parking: Higher-end or busy restaurants may offer valet parking services where a valet attendant parks customers' cars for them. This can be convenient for customers but may come with an additional fee.
Parking Garages/Lots: Restaurants in urban areas or commercial districts may be located near public parking garages or lots where customers can park for a fee.
Public Transportation Access: In areas well-served by public transportation, restaurants may not offer parking but instead encourage customers to use public transit.

Hygiene and Sanitation: Regular cleaning and disinfection of all surfaces, including tables, chairs, menus, door handles, and restrooms. Use of food-grade sanitizers and disinfectants is essential.
Hand Hygiene: Encouraging frequent handwashing among staff and providing hand sanitizing stations for both staff and customers. Staff should wash their hands thoroughly and frequently, especially after handling food, money, or other high-touch surfaces.
Food Safety Practices: Adherence to strict food safety guidelines, including proper storage, handling, and cooking of food to prevent contamination and foodborne illnesses. Regular training of staff on food safety protocols is crucial.
Physical Distancing: Arranging tables and seating areas to ensure adequate distance between diners, following local health guidelines or regulations. Implementing measures such as spaced seating, partitions, or barriers between tables can help maintain physical distancing.
Mask Policies: Following local regulations or guidelines regarding mask-wearing for both staff and customers. Some restaurants may require staff to wear masks at all times and enforce mask-wearing for customers when they are not seated at their tables.
Ventilation: Ensuring proper ventilation in indoor dining areas to minimize the spread of airborne particles. This may include opening windows or doors, using air purifiers, or upgrading HVAC systems to improve air circulation and filtration.
Health Screening: Implementing health screening measures for staff, such as temperature checks and symptom monitoring, to identify and prevent sick employees from working.
Contactless Options: Offering contactless payment options, digital menus, and online ordering for takeout or delivery to minimize physical contact between staff and customers.
Training and Communication: Providing ongoing training and communication to staff about safety protocols and best practices to ensure compliance and awareness.
Compliance with Regulations: Staying informed about and complying with local health regulations and guidelines to ensure the safety and well-being of customers and staff.
